# Retaining Wall Subassembly: Codes and Surfaces

This document outlines the Point, Link, and Shape codes generated by the retaining wall subassembly, and how they map to Corridor Surfaces for volume calculations.

## Point Codes
* `RW_Attach`: The initial origin point (0,0).
* `RW_TopStemFront`, `RW_TopToe`, `RW_TopHeel`, `RW_NotchTop`: Upper perimeter points.
* `RW_FootingToe`, `RW_FootingHeel`: Base of the concrete structure.
* `RW_PccLeft`, `RW_PccRight`, `RW_PccBottomLeft`, `RW_PccBottomRight`: Corners of the lean concrete mud mat.
* `RW_FilterTopRight`, `RW_FilterBottomLeft`, `RW_FilterBottomRight`: Corners of the drainage backfill.

## Link Codes
Link codes establish the boundaries for corridor surfaces.

* `RW_Top` / `Top`: Top links of the concrete wall, filter, and footing. Used for finished level surfaces.
* `RW_Datum` / `Datum`: Bottom-most links of the structure, PCC, and filter media. Used for earthwork and excavation calculations.
* `RW_Wall`: Vertical or near-vertical concrete faces. **Note**: Generally excluded from TIN surfaces to prevent bad triangulation.
* `RW_Footing`: Internal structural links for the footing.
* `RW_PCC`: Perimeter of the lean concrete mat.
* `RW_Filter`: Perimeter of the drainage media.

## Shape Codes
Shape codes are used for computing material volume quantities (QTO).
* `Structure_Concrete`
* `PCC_Lean_Concrete`
* `Filter_Drainage_Media`

## Corridor Surface Workflows
You can automatically create surfaces using the custom command `RW_CREATE_SURFACES`, which handles:
1.  **`RW_Top_Surface`**: Built from the `Top` link codes.
2.  **`RW_Earthwork_Surface`**: Built from the `Datum` link codes. Used to compare against the Existing Ground Level (EGL) for cut/fill volumes.
3.  **`RW_Structure_Reference`**: Optional non-volume surface built from the concrete and PCC links.
